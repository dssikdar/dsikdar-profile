About

AI Reasoning for Science

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student at UC San Diego, advised by Prof. Lianhui Qin. I am interested in machine learning methods for biological data, especially settings where images, gene expression, spatial information, and text need to be considered together.

My recent work has involved histopathology images, single-cell and spatial transcriptomics, and multimodal generative models. I try to build tools that are useful for analyzing biological structure while staying close to the underlying data.

Before UCSD, I completed my B.S. in Computer Science with a specialization in Intelligent Systems at the University of California, Irvine, where I worked in Zhang Laboratory on vision-omics learning, tissue morphology generation, and cell-cell communication analysis.
